Inventory notes:

- `/dev`: Alpine OpenRC `devfs` expects `/dev`, `/dev/console`,
  `/dev/null`, `/dev/tty`, `/dev/ttyS0`, `/dev/zero`, `/dev/random`, and
  `/dev/urandom`; optional `/dev/fd`, `/dev/stdin`, `/dev/stdout`,
  `/dev/stderr`, and `/dev/core` are created by scripts when their procfs
  targets exist.
- `/proc`: early scripts read `/proc/filesystems`, `/proc/mounts`,
  `/proc/self/mounts`, `/proc/cmdline`, `/proc/devices`, `/proc/modules`,
  `/proc/self/fd`, `/proc/self/exe`, `/proc/self/cmdline`,
  `/proc/self/stat*`, `/proc/<pid>/mountinfo`, and `/proc/<pid>/cgroup`.
  Optional cgroup, binfmt_misc, xenfs, ACPI, PMU, and module surfaces remain
  unadvertised unless a later slice implements those subsystems.
- `/sys`: early scripts require `/sys`, `/sys/class/tty`, and
  `/sys/devices/system/cpu` probes; optional securityfs, debugfs, tracefs,
  configfs, fusectl, selinuxfs, pstore, bpf, and cgroup mounts remain
  unadvertised.
- `/run`, `/tmp`, and `/var/tmp`: OpenRC requires these to exist as writable
  volatile filesystems before service state and temporary files are created.
