Bring up BPI-F3 PCIe and M.2 support.

Status:

- Planned.  Start after the PCI/PCIe server exploration and the first BPI-F3
  hardware boot milestone.

Problem:

- BPI-F3 exposes PCIe/M.2 hardware.  Bunix needs firmware-DT-driven PCIe host
  bridge discovery, ECAM/config-space access, MSI or legacy interrupt routing,
  and resource-window management before M.2 devices can be safely enumerated.

Tasks:

- [ ] Capture PCIe host bridge, address window, interrupt, reset, clock, PHY,
      and regulator DT nodes from BPI-F3.
- [ ] Confirm whether the firmware exposes ECAM-compatible config space or a
      vendor-specific access path.
- [ ] Extend the PCI/PCIe server to consume the BPI-F3 host bridge description.
- [ ] Bring up read-only PCI config-space enumeration.
- [ ] Implement resource window allocation and BAR mapping through
      capabilities.
- [ ] Bring up interrupt delivery for PCIe devices.
- [ ] Prove enumeration of a known M.2 endpoint.
- [ ] Split NVMe, WiFi, or other endpoint drivers into separate explorations.
