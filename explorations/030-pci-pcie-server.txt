Factor PCI/PCIe functionality out of VirtIO into a dedicated server.

Problem:

- `virtio-bus.server` currently owns both VirtIO device policy and low-level
  x86_64 PCI/PCIe discovery/configuration details.  That was pragmatic for
  early VirtIO bringup, but it conflates two buses and makes future transports
  harder to reason about.
- The upcoming riscv64 work should not inherit an x86_64 PCI-shaped VirtIO
  stack.  RISC-V boards may use VirtIO MMIO, platform-specific PCIe host
  controllers, firmware-described ECAM windows, or no PCIe at the first
  milestone.
- A dedicated PCI/PCIe server gives Bunix a clearer hardware-resource boundary:
  PCI discovers functions and grants narrowly-scoped config/BAR/MSI resources;
  VirtIO consumes either PCI resources or future MMIO resources through a bus
  neutral device-facing API.

Scheduling:

- Run this before `031-multiserver-usb-stack.txt` and
  `032-riscv64-initial-port.txt`.
- Keep x86_64 VirtIO block/network tests passing throughout the refactor.
- The riscv64 port can then implement only the platform discovery/transport
  pieces it actually needs, without untangling PCI assumptions from VirtIO.

Design direction:

- Introduce a userspace PCI/PCIe server, likely `pci.server`, with a FourCC
  protocol and service name such as `PCI0`.
- Move x86_64 PCI config-port scanning, BAR sizing, BAR capability grants,
  command-register setup, and VirtIO PCI capability parsing out of
  `virtio-bus.server` where they are generic PCI concerns.
- Keep VirtIO-specific feature negotiation, virtqueue setup, notify handling,
  and device-class binding in `virtio-bus.server`, but make the PCI transport
  an explicit provider below it.
- Preserve OCAP boundaries: the kernel grants raw hardware resources to the PCI
  server; PCI grants attenuated device resources to consumers; drivers never
  receive broad I/O or MMIO authority.

Constraints:

- Do not move PCI policy into the kernel.  Kernel involvement should remain
  narrow hardware-resource validation and grant creation.
- Do not hardcode QEMU-only topology into the new protocol.  The first backend
  can scan bus 0 with config ports, but the interface should be able to grow
  ECAM, PCIe root complexes, bridges, and firmware-provided windows.
- Keep device enumeration deterministic enough for tests.
- Preserve existing `test-boot-virtio`, `test-boot-virtio-blk`, and
  `test-boot-virtio-net` behavior while changing ownership under the hood.
