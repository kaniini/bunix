Plan BPI-F3 WiFi and Bluetooth support.

Status:

- Postponed.  Do not start BPI-F3-specific WiFi/Bluetooth work until the
  generic riscv64 port boots an Alpine environment in QEMU, exploration 070 has
  been explicitly resumed, and USB, PCIe/M.2, and core networking are stable
  enough to identify how the board's wireless hardware is attached.

Problem:

- BPI-F3 variants may expose WiFi/Bluetooth through a module or expansion path
  that depends on PCIe, SDIO, USB, UART, GPIO, regulators, and firmware blobs.
  Bunix needs a clear attachment and firmware policy before attempting wireless
  bringup.

Tasks:

- [ ] Identify the exact WiFi/Bluetooth hardware present on the target board
      variant.
- [ ] Capture the relevant DT nodes, GPIOs, regulators, clocks, UART, SDIO,
      USB, or PCIe attachment data.
- [ ] Decide project policy for proprietary firmware blobs in userspace or
      rootfs images.
- [ ] Bring up Bluetooth UART or USB transport if present.
- [ ] Bring up WiFi transport after the underlying bus and network stack are
      stable.
- [ ] Add observability for radio power, firmware load, link association, and
      packet counters.
